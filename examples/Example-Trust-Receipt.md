# Example Trust Receipt

## Overview

This example demonstrates how ProofSettle generates a **Trust Receipt** after a successful operational decision and trustless settlement.

Unlike a traditional transaction receipt, a Trust Receipt provides a transparent, explainable, and independently verifiable record of the complete decision journey.

Its purpose is to help users understand **how an operational decision was produced**, not simply confirm that settlement occurred.

# What Can Be Verified?

A Trust Receipt enables independent verification of:

- The sporting event that triggered the decision.
- The verified sports intelligence used during evaluation.
- The operational decision lifecycle.
- Rule evaluation completion.
- Decision validation.
- Cryptographic integrity.
- Settlement authorization.
- Final trustless settlement.

---

# Why Trust Receipts Matter

Traditional settlement systems typically answer one question:

> **"Did settlement happen?"**

ProofSettle answers a more important question:

> **"How was the operational decision that led to settlement actually produced?"**

The Trust Receipt provides visibility into the complete operational decision journey, allowing anyone to understand, inspect, and independently verify the process behind the final outcome.

# Conclusion

A Trust Receipt is not simply a confirmation of settlement.

It is the user's transparency window into the complete operational decision journey.

By preserving the journey—from verified sports intelligence through rule evaluation, validation, cryptographic integrity, and settlement—ProofSettle enables trust to be earned through transparency rather than assumed through automation.
