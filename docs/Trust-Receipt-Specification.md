# Trust Receipt Specification

## Version 1.0

---

# Overview

The Trust Receipt is the final transparency artifact generated by ProofSettle after an operational decision has successfully completed its decision lifecycle and trustless settlement.

Unlike traditional settlement receipts that merely confirm a completed transaction, a Trust Receipt provides a transparent, explainable, and independently verifiable record of **how the operational decision was produced**.

Its purpose is to help users, operators, auditors, regulators, and future intelligent systems understand the complete decision journey.

---

# Purpose

A Trust Receipt exists to answer one simple question:

> **"How can I trust that the operational decision genuinely followed a transparent and trustworthy procedure?"**

Rather than asking users to blindly trust the system, ProofSettle enables anyone to inspect the complete decision lifecycle.

---

# Design Principles

Every Trust Receipt is designed around five principles.

## Transparency

The operational decision should be understandable.

---

## Explainability

The decision journey should clearly communicate how the final decision was reached.

---

## Verifiability

Every recorded stage should be independently verifiable.

---

## Integrity

Cryptographic references preserve the integrity of recorded decision information.

---

## Permanence

The Trust Receipt becomes a permanent historical record of the completed decision lifecycle.

# Trust Receipt Structure

Every Trust Receipt contains five logical sections.

## 1. Decision Information

Records the operational decision.

Typical fields include:

- Decision Identifier
- Decision Type
- Decision Status
- Timestamp

---

## 2. Match Information

Provides contextual information.

Typical fields include:

- Competition
- Match Identifier
- Teams
- Final Score
- Outcome

---

## 3. Decision Journey

Documents the transparent operational workflow.

Typical stages include:

- Verified Sports Intelligence
- Decision Intelligence
- Smart Contract Rule Evaluation
- Decision Validation
- Decision Artifact
- Cryptographic Verification
- Settlement Authorization
- Trustless Settlement

---

## 4. Cryptographic References

Preserves integrity information.

Examples include:

- Decision Artifact Identifier
- Artifact Hash
- Merkle Root
- Verification Hash

These references enable independent verification of recorded information.

---

## 5. Settlement Information

Records settlement completion.

Typical fields include:

- Network
- Transaction Identifier
- Settlement Status
- Settlement Timestamp
